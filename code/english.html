    Grammar

    1. Parts of Speech

    a. Nouns

    Definition: Names of people, places, things, or ideas.

    • People: teacher, John
    • Places: school, London
    • Things: book, car
    • Ideas: happiness, courage
    Examples:

    The dog is barking.

    She lives in New York.

    b. Pronouns

    Definition: Words that replace nouns.

    Types: Personal (he, she, it), Possessive (his, hers), Reflexive (myself, yourself).

    Examples:

    John is my friend. He is kind.

    c. Verbs

    Definition: Action or state of being.

    Types: Action Verbs (run, dance), Linking Verbs (is, are), Auxiliary Verbs (have, will).

    Examples:

    She sings beautifully.

    They are happy.

    d. Adjectives

    Definition: Describe nouns or pronouns.

    Examples:

    The beautiful flower is blooming.

    He is a smart student.

    e. Adverbs

    Definition: Modify verbs, adjectives, or other adverbs.

    Examples:

    She speaks softly.

    He runs very fast.

    f. Prepositions

    Definition: Show relationships between nouns/pronouns and other words.

    Examples:

    The book is on the table.

    She went to the market.

    g. Conjunctions

    Definition: Connect words, phrases, or clauses.

    Types: Coordinating (and, but), Subordinating (because, although).

    Examples:

    I like tea and coffee.

    He left because it was late.

    h. Interjections

    Definition: Express emotions.

    Examples:

    Wow! That‚Äôs amazing.

    Ouch! That hurt.

    2. Sentence Structure

    a. Subject + Predicate

    Definition:

    Subject: Who or what the sentence is about.

    Predicate: What the subject does or is.

    Examples:

    She (subject) is reading a book (predicate).

    b. Types of Sentences
    • Declarative: I like ice cream.
    • Interrogative: Do you like pizza?
    • Imperative: Close the door.
    • Exclamatory: Wow! That‚Äôs great.

    3. Tenses

    a. Present Tense
    • Simple Present: She plays guitar.
    • Present Continuous: She is playing guitar.
    • Present Perfect: She has played guitar.
    • Present Perfect Continuous: She has been playing guitar for an hour.
    b. Past Tense
    • Simple Past: She played guitar.
    • Past Continuous: She was playing guitar.
    • Past Perfect: She had played guitar.
    • Past Perfect Continuous: She had been playing guitar for an hour.
    c. Future Tense
    • Simple Future: She will play guitar.
    • Future Continuous: She will be playing guitar.
    • Future Perfect: She will have played guitar.
    • Future Perfect Continuous: She will have been playing guitar for an hour.

    4. Articles

    Definition: Words used before nouns (a, an, the).

    Examples:

    A dog is barking.

    An apple a day keeps the doctor away.

    The moon is bright tonight.

    5. Active and Passive Voice

    Active: The subject performs the action (She writes a letter).

    Passive: The action is done to the subject (A letter is written by her).

    6. Direct and Indirect Speech

    Direct: Exact words (He said, ‚ÄúI am tired‚Äù).

    Indirect: Reported words (He said that he was tired).

    7. Punctuation

    • Period (.)
    • Comma (,)
    • Question Mark (?)
    • Exclamation Mark (!)
    • Quotation Marks (" ")
    Examples:

    ‚ÄúHello!‚Äù she said.

    Are you coming?

    8. Subject-Verb Agreement

    Definition: The subject and verb must agree in number (singular/plural).

    Examples:

    He runs fast.

    They run fast.

    9. Modals

    Definition: Helping verbs expressing necessity, possibility, etc. (can, could, may, might, must, shall, should, will, would).

    Examples:

    You must do your homework.

    She can sing well.

    10. Clauses and Phrases

    a. Phrase

    A group of words without a subject and verb (on the table).

    b. Clause

    A group of words with a subject and verb (She runs fast).

    c. Types of Clauses
    • Independent: I was late.
    • Dependent: Because I missed the bus.

    11. Figures of Speech

    • Simile: As brave as a lion.
    • Metaphor: Time is money.
    • Personification: The wind whispered.
    • Hyperbole: I‚Äôve told you a million times.

    12. Conditional Sentences

    • Zero Conditional: If you heat water, it boils.
    • First Conditional: If it rains, we will stay inside.
    • Second Conditional: If I were rich, I would travel the world.
    • Third Conditional: If I had studied, I would have passed.
