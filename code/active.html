What is Active Recall?

Active recall is a powerful technique that helps improve your ability to retain information by recalling it from memory. Rather than passively reviewing notes, you actively test yourself on the material to strengthen memory pathways.

How to Practice Active Recall

To use active recall effectively, follow these steps:

  • Study a topic thoroughly first.
  • After studying, close your books and try to recall the material without looking at your notes.
  • Write down everything you remember, then check your notes to fill in the gaps.
  • Repeat the process regularly to reinforce memory retention.

Why Active Recall Works

Active recall works because it forces your brain to actively retrieve information, making the memory stronger. This is far more effective than simply rereading or passive studying.
